Optical Model Potential Implementation

A detailed walk-through of how the Koning-Delaroche optical model replaces the constant matrix element for pre-equilibrium internal transition rates.

Why Replace M² with an OMP?

In the standard exciton model, the internal transition rate for pair creation is proportional to a constant matrix element \(M^2\) (Kalbach-Cline parametrization). While computationally simple, this approach ignores the energy and isospin dependence of the nucleon-nucleon interaction inside the nuclear medium. The optical model potential (OMP) provides a more physically motivated collision probability derived from the imaginary part of the nucleon-nucleus interaction.

Key insight: The imaginary part of the optical potential \(W(r)\) describes the absorption of flux from the elastic channel into more complex configurations — exactly the physics needed for pre-equilibrium internal transitions.

The Koning-Delaroche Global Optical Model

The KD03 potential [1] is the current standard global phenomenological optical potential, fitted to a large database of elastic scattering observables. The full potential has the form:

\[ U(r) = V(r) + iW(r) + V_{so}(r)(\mathbf{l}\cdot\mathbf{s}) + iW_{so}(r)(\mathbf{l}\cdot\mathbf{s}) + V_C(r) \]

For the exciton model, we need only the central imaginary potential \(W(r) = W_v(r) + W_d(r)\). The spin-orbit contribution averages to zero over the Fermi sphere and is not needed for absorption-strength calculations.

Energy Dependence of the Imaginary Potential

The volume and surface imaginary strengths depend on the fragment kinetic energy E (per nucleon):

\[ W_v(E) = n \cdot W_1^n \frac{(E_{\text{eff}} - E_f^n)^2} {(E_{\text{eff}} - E_f^n)^2 + (W_2^n)^2} + z \cdot W_1^p \frac{(E_{\text{eff}} - E_f^p)^2} {(E_{\text{eff}} - E_f^p)^2 + (W_2^p)^2} \]
\[ W_d(E) = n \cdot D_1^n \frac{(E_{\text{eff}} - E_f^n)^2 e^{-D_2^n (E_{\text{eff}} - E_f^n)}} {(E_{\text{eff}} - E_f^n)^2 + (D_3^n)^2} + z \cdot D_1^p \frac{(E_{\text{eff}} - E_f^p)^2 e^{-D_2^p (E_{\text{eff}} - E_f^p)}} {(E_{\text{eff}} - E_f^p)^2 + (D_3^p)^2} \]

where \(n\) and \(z\) are the neutron and proton numbers of the fragment, and \(E_{\text{eff}} = E/a\) for fragment mass \(a\).

Well Depth Averaging

The radius and diffuseness parameters are averaged over the fragment composition:

\[ R_v = \frac{n R_v^n + z R_v^p}{a},\quad a_v = \frac{n a_v^n + z a_v^p}{a},\quad R_d = \frac{n R_d^n + z R_d^p}{a},\quad a_d = \frac{n a_d^n + z a_d^p}{a} \]

The Volume-Averaged Imaginary Potential

The radial form of the imaginary potential at radius \(r\) is:

\[ W(r) = W_v f_v(r) - 4 a_d W_d \frac{df_s}{dr} \]

where \(f_v\) and \(f_s\) are Woods-Saxon form factors. The volume average is:

\[ \langle W \rangle = \frac{\int_0^\infty W(r) f_v(r) r^2 dr} {\int_0^\infty f_v(r) r^2 dr} \]

Following TALYS bonetti.f90, this is evaluated with a 50-bin midpoint rule at dr = 0.4 fm.

The Wompfac: Bridging M² and the OMP

To maintain consistency with existing TALYS parameterizations while replacing the absolute scale, the code uses a weighting factor (wompfac) that preserves the relative channel strengths from M²:

\[ \begin{aligned} \text{wompfac}_{\text{same}} &= \frac{M^2_c \cdot 0.55}{1 + 2R_{\pi\nu}/R_{\pi\pi}} \\[4pt] \text{wompfac}_{\text{cross}} &= \frac{M^2_c \cdot 0.55 \cdot 2R_{\pi\nu}/R_{\pi\pi}}{1 + 2R_{\pi\nu}/R_{\pi\pi}} \\[4pt] \text{wompfac}_{\text{conv}} &= \frac{\text{wompfac}_{\text{same}} + \text{wompfac}_{\text{cross}}}{2} \end{aligned} \]

The constant \(C_{\text{OMP}} = 0.55\) comes from TALYS's matrix.f90. The collision rate in the integrand becomes:

\[ \lambda_{\text{collision}} = \frac{2}{\hbar} \cdot \text{wompfac} \cdot \langle W \rangle \]

Data Flow

Kinematics: From Integration Variable to Kinetic Energy

Inside the numerical integrand, the integration variable \(e\) is the energy of the created particle-hole pair. The kinetic energy of the colliding nucleon is:

\[ e_{\text{kin}} = e - S_{p/n}(Z_{\text{comp}}, N_{\text{comp}}) \]

where \(S_p\) and \(S_n\) are the proton and neutron separation energies from the MARLEY MassTable. The kinetic energy is clamped to ≥ -20 MeV to avoid singularities in the KD model at very negative energies.

Hole-Channel Ratio

In the OMP integrand, the hole-channel ratio \(\omega_{\text{hole}}(e)/\omega_{\text{particle}}(e)\) is retained. This purely statistical factor reflects the probability that the created hole configuration is populated — it is independent of the collision mechanism.
