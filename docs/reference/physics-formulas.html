Physics Formulas

Key equations from the TALYS manual (Chapter 13) implemented in this codebase.

1. Single-Particle State Densities

Ericson's formula (Eq. 13.5):

\[ g_\pi = \frac{Z}{15}, \qquad g_\nu = \frac{N}{15} \]

Code: spDensityProton() / spDensityNeutron() in src/preeq_common.cpp.

2. Particle–Hole State Density

Eq. 13.4 (Williams formula, two-component):

\[ \omega(p_\pi, h_\pi, p_\nu, h_\nu, E_x) = \frac{g_\pi^{p_\pi + h_\pi} g_\nu^{p_\nu + h_\nu}} {p_\pi!\, h_\pi!\, p_\nu!\, h_\nu!\, (n-1)!} \, U^{n-1} \, f_{\text{Pauli}} \cdot f_{\text{well}} \cdot f_{\text{pairing}} \cdot f_{\text{parity}} \]

Code: particleHoleStateDensity() in src/state_density.cpp:17.

3. Pauli Correction

Eq. 13.9:

\[ P(p_\pi, h_\pi, p_\nu, h_\nu) = \frac{1}{4} \left[ \frac{p_\pi(p_\pi-1) + h_\pi(h_\pi-1)}{g_\pi} + \frac{p_\nu(p_\nu-1) + h_\nu(h_\nu-1)}{g_\nu} + 2\frac{p_\pi h_\pi + p_\nu h_\nu + p_\pi h_\nu + p_\nu h_\pi}{g_\pi + g_\nu} \right] \]

Code: pauliCorrection() in src/preeq_common.cpp.

4. Finite Well Correction

Eq. 13.16:

\[ f_{\text{well}}(p, h, U, V) = 1 - \left(\frac{U}{U+V}\right)^{p+h} \]

Code: finiteWell() in src/preeq_common.cpp.

5. Pairing Energy Correction

Back-shifted pairing energy (Eq. 13.10–13.13):

\[ \Delta_{\chi} = \chi \frac{12}{\sqrt{A}}, \qquad n_{\text{crit}} = 2 \sqrt{\frac{g_\pi g_\nu}{g_\pi + g_\nu}} \, \Delta \] \[ P_{ph}(E_x, n, n_{\text{crit}}) = \begin{cases} 1 & n \ge n_{\text{crit}} \\ \exp\!\left(-\frac{n_{\text{crit}} - n}{n_{\text{crit}} - 1} \cdot \frac{\Delta}{U}\right) & n < n_{\text{crit}} \end{cases} \]

Code: pairingEnergyDelta(), nCrit(), fuPairingCorrection() in src/preeq_common.cpp.

6. Matrix Element M²

Eq. 13.26 (Kalbach–Cline parametrization):

\[ M^2 = C_1 \frac{A_p}{A^3} \left[ 7.48 C_2 + \frac{4.62 \times 10^5}{(10.7 C_3 + E_{\text{tot}}/(n A_p))^3} \right] \]

Analytical mode includes an additional factor of 1.20 (TALYS preeqmode=1 convention).

Channel ratios (Eq. 13.24):

\[ M_{\pi\pi}^2 = R_{\pi\pi} M^2,\quad M_{\nu\nu}^2 = R_{\nu\nu} M^2,\quad M_{\pi\nu}^2 = R_{\pi\nu} M^2,\quad M_{\nu\pi}^2 = R_{\nu\pi} M^2 \]

Default ratios: \(R_{\nu\nu}=1.5\), all others 1.0.

Code: M2() (transition_rates.cpp:10), M2_element() (transition_rates.cpp:24).

7. Transition Rates — Pair Creation (Numerical)

Eqs. 13.17–13.18:

\[ \lambda_{\pi^+}(p,h,E) = \frac{1}{\omega_i(U)} \sum_{\text{channels}} \int_{L_1}^{L_2} de\, \frac{2\pi}{\hbar} M_{\text{ch}}^2\, \omega_t(e)\, g_{\text{factor}}\, \omega_r(U-e) \]

Code: lambdaNewPairNumerical() in transition_rates.cpp:117.

8. Transition Rates — Conversion (Numerical)

Eq. 13.19:

\[ \lambda_{\pi\nu}(p,h,E) = \frac{1}{\omega_i(U)} \int_{L_1}^{L_2} de\, \frac{2\pi}{\hbar} M_{\pi\nu}^2\, \omega_c(e)\, \omega_a(e)\, \omega_r(U-e) \]

where \(\omega_c\) is the created state, \(\omega_a\) is the annihilated state, and \(\omega_r\) is the residual state densities.

Code: lambdaPairConversionNumerical() in transition_rates.cpp:310.

9. OMP Wompfac (preeqmode=3)

TALYS weighting factors bridging M² to the optical model:

\[ \text{wompfac}_{\text{same}} = \frac{M_c^2 \cdot 0.55}{1 + 2 R_{\pi\nu}/\text{R}_{\pi\pi}} \] \[ \text{wompfac}_{\text{cross}} = \frac{M_c^2 \cdot 0.55 \cdot 2 R_{\pi\nu}/\text{R}_{\pi\pi}}{1 + 2 R_{\pi\nu}/\text{R}_{\pi\pi}} \] \[ \text{wompfac}_{\text{conv}} = \frac{\text{wompfac}_{\text{same}} + \text{wompfac}_{\text{cross}}}{2} \]

Code: transition_rates.cpp:132-140 (pair creation), transition_rates.cpp:444-453 (conversion).

10. Volume-Averaged Imaginary Potential

Eqs. 13.29–13.30:

\[ W(r) = W_v f_v(r) - 4 a_d W_d \frac{d}{dr} f_s(r) \] \[ \langle W \rangle = \frac{\int_0^\infty W(r) f_v(r) r^2 dr}{\int_0^\infty f_v(r) r^2 dr} \]

Evaluated with a 50-bin midpoint rule at dr = 0.4 fm.

Code: wvolRadialIntegral() in transition_rates.cpp:47-68 (OMP branch).

11. Spin Distribution

\[ R_n(J) = \frac{2J+1}{2\sqrt{2\pi\sigma_n^2}} \exp\!\left(-\frac{(J+\frac12)^2}{2\sigma_n^2}\right) \]

where \(\sigma_n^2 = 0.28 n A^{2/3}\) (TALYS-compatible).

Code: spinDistribution() in src/preeq_common.cpp.
